4. Key Observations

SAT reward drives consistency improvement

SAT-trained models show steadily increasing consistency scores (DeepSeek-7B SAT reaches 0.9+), demonstrating that explicit symbolic verification pushes models toward logically valid reasoning chains.

Differentiable NSFR enables smoother learning

Continuous [0,1] feedback from the differentiable NSFR reasoner produces smoother reward curves compared to the binary SAT signal. This is especially visible in the DeepSeek-7B and Mistral-7B runs.

Answer-only baseline stagnates

Without a consistency signal, models plateau quickly on total reward. The answer-only runs consistently underperform SAT and Diff-Anneal variants across all model families.

SFT warmup critical for think format

SAT-Think runs (with SFT pre-training) start with functional format compliance that rapidly improves, while cold-start runs begin with low format scores and need many steps to recover.

KL divergence requires monitoring

DeepSeek-7B SAT-Think shows KL explosion (>1000) early in training, and Mistral-7B Diff-Anneal reaches KL >500 late in training. These events correlate with reward degradation and require early stopping or KL penalty tuning.
